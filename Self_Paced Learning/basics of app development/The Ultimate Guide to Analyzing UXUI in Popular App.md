## A. The 10 Usability Heuristics (Nielsen) Applied
To truly analyze these apps, we must apply Jakob Nielsenâ€™s 10 Heuristics. Let's map them to our apps:

1.  **Visibility of System Status:**

    *   *App:* **Bayut**.

    *   *App:* **Gmail / WhatsApp**.
    *   *Analysis:* The "Undo Send" or "Delete for Everyone" feature. Users make mistakes; good UX provides an "Emergency Exit."
4.  **Consistency and Standards:**
    *   *App:* **Facebook**.
    *   *Analysis:* The "Like" icon is always a thumbs up. The notification bell is always top right. Consistency reduces learning curves.
5.  **Error Prevention:**
    *   *App:* **Emirates NBD**.

6.  **Recognition Rather Than Recall:**
    *   *App:* **Zomato**.
    *   *Analysis:* Showing "Recent Searches" or "Order Again." The user doesn't have to remember the name of that sushi place; the app remembers for them.
7.  **Flexibility and Efficiency of Use:**
    *   *App:* **Instagram**.
    *   *Analysis:* Double tap to like (Accelerator) vs. hitting the heart button (Novice). Good UI caters to both.
8.  **Aesthetic and Minimalist Design:**
    *   *App:* **TikTok**.
    *   *Analysis:* No clutter. Just video. Every extra unit of information competes with the relevant units of information.
9.  **Help Users Recognize, Diagnose, and Recover from Errors:**
    *   *App:* **Login Screens**.
    *   *Analysis:* "Incorrect Password" in red text. Even better: "Did you forget your password? Reset here."
10. **Help and Documentation:**
    *   *App:* **Settings Menus**.

## C. Gestalt Principles in UI ğŸ§ 
How our brains group things.
*   **Proximity:** Things close together are related. (e.g., The price of the house in Bayut is placed right next to the photo of the house).

*   **Closure:** We fill in the gaps. (e.g., An icon that is a partial circle is perceived as a circle).
*   **Continuity:** The eye follows a path. (e.g., The vertical list in WhatsApp guides the eye down).
